Area of Impact: Training & Simulation

Training for war without firing a shot.

Modern armed forces rehearse missions, fly fighter jets and command entire battles inside computer-generated worlds. This is the story of military simulation — the technology that lets soldiers, pilots and commanders make their mistakes in a virtual world so they don't make them on a real battlefield.

Definition Military simulation is the use of computer models, virtual reality, motion platforms and war-gaming software to recreate combat conditions for training, mission rehearsal, weapons testing and strategic decision-making — without the cost, danger or environmental damage of using real troops, vehicles and ammunition.
