How it's used today

Military simulation is not science fiction — it is used every single day by the world's armed forces. Here are the real applications, who uses them, and why they have become essential.

// Real-world applications

Where it's used right now

Tap a tab to explore each area.

Pilot training & mission rehearsal

Fast-jet pilots may complete a large share of their early training in full-motion simulators before ever flying the real aircraft. Crews rehearse dangerous scenarios — engine failure, enemy missiles, night landings — endlessly, with no risk. Aircraft like the F-35 Lightning II were designed with simulation in mind, because flying the real jet for every lesson would be extraordinarily expensive.

Who uses it: the US Air Force, RAF, and the Royal Australian Air Force (RAAF), among many others.

Soldier & vehicle crew training

Armies use systems like VBS (Virtual Battlespace) and the US Army's Synthetic Training Environment (STE) to train infantry squads, drivers and gunners. Soldiers practise patrols, room-clearing, convoy ambushes and rules-of-engagement decisions in a virtual town before deploying. Laser-based "live" systems (like MILES) let real soldiers safely "shoot" each other in field exercises.

Ship & submarine operations

Bridge simulators recreate ship handling, navigation in storms and busy ports, while combat-system trainers rehearse detecting and responding to threats. Submarine crews train on sonar and damage-control simulators because real sea time is scarce and costly.

Combat medical training

Medics use haptic mannequins and VR trauma simulators to practise treating battlefield injuries under realistic stress — controlling bleeding, clearing airways and triaging casualties — so their first real patient is not their first attempt.

Command, staff war-gaming & planning

Officers at staff colleges use constructive simulations (such as JCATS and OneSAF) to move thousands of virtual units and test entire battle plans. This "war-gaming" reveals weaknesses in a strategy before any real operation begins, and is also used to plan logistics and humanitarian/disaster responses.

Programs you can look up

🇺🇸

Red Flag & the STE (USA)

The US runs huge "Red Flag" combat exercises blending real flights with simulation, and is building the Synthetic Training Environment to unify all training under one system.

🇦🇺

Australian Defence Force

The ADF operates flight simulators for the F-35 and other aircraft, and invests in simulation centres to train personnel efficiently across a vast, sparsely-populated country.

🛡️

NATO joint exercises

NATO uses distributed (networked) simulation so allied nations can train together in one shared virtual battlespace despite being in different countries.

🧩

VBS by Bohemia Interactive

Built from the same studio behind the ARMA games, VBS is a purpose-made military trainer adopted by defence forces worldwide.
